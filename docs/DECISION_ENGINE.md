# Syntrix Decision Engine

## Mission

Help operators make faster, better decisions through explainable operational intelligence.

---

# Philosophy

Syntrix is not a reporting platform.

Syntrix is not an AI chatbot.

Syntrix is an AI-powered operating system.

Every feature exists to improve one operational decision.

If a feature does not improve a decision, it does not belong in Syntrix.

---

# Decision Cycle

Detect

↓

Investigate

↓

Recommend

↓

Execute

↓

Verify

↓

Learn

Every screen strengthens one step of this cycle.

---

# Intelligence Contract

Every recommendation generated by Syntrix Intelligence must answer five questions.

1. What should I do?

2. Why?

3. What is the business impact?

4. How confident are we?

5. What evidence supports this?

If one of these questions cannot be answered, the recommendation is incomplete.

---

# Explainability

AI never replaces data.

AI explains data.

Every recommendation must be traceable back to operational evidence.

Users should always be able to understand why Syntrix reached a conclusion.

---

# Product Principles

Explain before visualizing.

Business impact before KPIs.

Confidence before recommendations.

Evidence before trust.

Actions before reports.

Decision quality over information quantity.

---

# Engineering Principles

Pages assemble features.

Features assemble UI.

UI never owns business logic.

Business logic belongs in lib/.

Data belongs in data/.

Name components after business concepts, not UI elements.

Keep files honest.

Don't generalize until a pattern exists.

---

# Success

A successful Syntrix screen reduces cognitive load.

Users should spend less time interpreting dashboards and more time making decisions.
